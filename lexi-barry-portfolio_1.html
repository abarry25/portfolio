Editor's letter

Designing for the moment someone finally knows what to do next.

Every project in this portfolio begins with the same question: where are people getting stuck? Sometimes the answer becomes a website, sometimes a planning tool, sometimes an educational experience, and occasionally a painting. Regardless of the medium, my work centers on reducing complexity and creating experiences that help people understand what comes next.

My background spans education, community building, digital product design, and software development. I've spent more than a decade designing learning experiences and operational systems for schools, nonprofits, entrepreneurs, and mission-driven organizations. This collection highlights projects that blend strategy, design, and technology to make complicated ideas feel approachable.

Case study — ux/ui design

Find Your Path — a wayfinding onboarding quiz for Harvard Innovation Labs

Rather than directing every founder toward the same list of opportunities, Find Your Path creates a personalized learning journey that helps founders identify the most relevant next step based on where they are today.

The brief

Turn a crowded catalog of programs into a five-minute path someone will actually finish.

The work

One question at a time, plain-language answer choices, visible progress, and a generated result instead of a static list.

The result

A single flow founders can complete in under two minutes and act on the same day.

Case study — planning & operations tool

i-lab planning timeline — one board for a whole year of programming

Every functional area of the i-lab needed a single place to stake out when its programs, cycles, and deadlines land, day by day or collapsed to weeks, with everyone looking at the same live grid instead of a dozen personal copies.

The problem

Programming for two terms lived across scattered docs with no shared source of truth.

The build

A collaborative grid with day and week views, inline editing, and per-row ownership.

The payoff

One-click export of marked rows to CSV or ClickUp, ready for import.
